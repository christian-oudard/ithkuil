# Chapter 1: Phonology

**A GRAMMAR OF NEW ITHKUIL - A CONSTRUCTED LANGUAGE**

## 1.0 Introduction

The phonology of a language refers to its system of vocally articulated sounds and how those sounds are sequentially combined to build words.

## 1.1 Phonemes

The phonemes of a language are the meaningful vocally articulated sounds used to form words.

## 1.2 Pronunciation

### 1.2.1 Vowels

- **a** - [a] as in Spanish _alta_
- **ä** - [æ], as in American English _cat_
- **e** - [ɛ] as in English _let_, or [e] in Spanish, but only [e] at the beginning of a bivocalic conjunct
- **ë** - [ʌ] like the _u_ in English _cut_, or the "schwa" sound [ə] like the _a_ in English _sofa_
- **i** - [i] as in Spanish or Italian, or [ɪ] as in English _pit_
- **o** - [ɔ] like the first o in Italian _otto_, or [o] as in Spanish
- **ö** - [œ] as in French _neuf_ or [ø] as in French _feu_
- **u** - [ʊ] as in English _book_, or [u] as in Spanish
- **ü** - [ʉ] as in Swedish _hus_ or [y] as in French _lune_

### Diphthongs

The permissible diphthongs (all are "falling") are: **ai**, **ei**, **ëi**, **oi**, **ui**, **au**, **eu**, **ëu**, **ou**, and **iu**.

### 1.2.2 Consonants

- **p** and **k** - unaspirated [p] and [k] as in French, Spanish, or Italian
- **t** - unaspirated and dental (not alveolar) [t̪] as in French, Spanish, or Italian
- **d** - dental (not alveolar) [d̪] as in French or Italian
- **g** - always the voiced velar stop [g] as in English _go_
- **h** - never silent, pronounced [h] in all positions
- **'** - glottal stop, as in _oh-oh!_
- **ţ** and **ḑ** - voiceless and voiced interdental fricatives [θ] and [ð] as in English _thin_ and _this_
- **š** and **ž** - voiceless and voiced alveolo-palatal fricatives [ʃ] and [ʒ], as in _mesh_ and _measure_
- **ç** - voiceless palatal slit-fricative [ç], as in German _richtig_
- **x** - velar or uvular voiceless fricative [x ~ χ], as in German _bach_
- **l** - non-velarized voiced apico-dental lateral approximant [l̪] as in French, Spanish, or Italian
- **ļ** - voiceless lateral fricative [ɬ] as in Welsh _llan_
- **c** and **ẓ** - voiceless and voiced apico-alveolar affricates [ʦ] and [ʣ] as in English _bits_ and _bids_
- **č** and **j** - voiceless and voiced affricates [ʧ] and [ʤ] as in English _butch_ and _budge_
- **n** - dental, not alveolar; assimilates to velar [ŋ] before **k**, **g**, and **x**
- **ň** - velar [ŋ] as in English _bring_
- **r** - single tap/flap [ɾ], becomes a trill [r] when geminated
- **ř** - voiced dorso-uvular approximant [ʁ] as in French _ire_ or German _uhr_
- **y** - voiced palatal approximant [j] as in English _yes_

The remaining consonants **b**, **f**, **m**, **s**, **v**, **w**, and **z** are pronounced as in English.

### Epenthetic Vowel

When a glottal stop (') is followed by a consonant, the glottal stop is usually followed by a very brief vowel sound. This vowel may be pronounced as [ɨ] (Russian быть) or [ɯ] (Turkish undotted i, Japanese short u).

## 1.3 Romanization Alternatives

- **ţ** may be written as **ṭ** or **ŧ**
- **ḑ** may be written as **ḍ** or **đ**
- **ň** may be written as **nļ** or **ṇ**
- **ř** may be written as **rļ** or **ṛ**
- **ļ** may be written as **ł** or **ḷ**

### 1.3.1 Optional Diacritics

- **ö** → **ô** (optional)
- **ì** - on unstressed **i** as initial member of vocalic conjunct (e.g., -Cìa-, -Cìo-) to distinguish from **Cy+V** syllables
- **ù** - on **u** to remind not to collapse to /w/

### 1.3.2 Syllable-boundary Markers

These are normally never written, given their occurrence depends on individual speech patterns.

## 1.4 Gemination

Consonantal gemination refers to the audible "doubling" in length of a consonant sound.

In New Ithkuil, most consonants can be geminated. All consonants except **w**, **y**, and the glottal stop are capable of gemination between two vowels. **w**, **y**, and stop consonants can be geminated in both word-initial and word-final position.

### Pronunciation of Geminated Consonants

- Continuants are pronounced for twice as long when geminated
- Geminated **r** is pronounced as a rapid apico-alveolar trill
- Stop consonants, when geminated, are momentarily held then released
- Affricates: if intervocalic, hold the initial stop before releasing; in word-initial/final position, lengthen the fricative portion

### Romanized Orthography

Single-character consonants are simply written double when geminated: **pp**, **tt**, **mm**, **cc**, **rr**, **ss**, etc.
